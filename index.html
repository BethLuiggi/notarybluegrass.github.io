Step 6 of 7 — Policies & Acknowledgment

1. Cancellations & Fees

Cancellations within less than 5 hours of scheduled time: 50% of fee charged. No-show or refusal to sign: full fee. Wait time: 15-min grace period, then $15 + $1/min. Re-draws due to lender error billed at signing rate.

2. Identification

In most cases, signers must present a valid non-expired passport, driver’s license, government-issued ID card, or other government-issued ID containing a signature and photograph. If unavailable, verification by oath of a credible witness personally appearing before the notary is an acceptable alternative.

3. Signatures

If physically unable to sign, you may direct another person (not the notary) to sign your name by proxy. This requires two additional witness signatures. Some signings require signature under oath witnessed by the notary; others only require notary verification. When in doubt, contact us before signing.

4. Powers of Attorney (POA)

If using a POA, it must be recorded at least 24 hours prior to use. The notary must see the POA for the signing to be valid.

5. Right to Refuse

The notary may refuse service if: (1) the person lacks the capacity to execute the record; (2) the signature does not appear knowingly and voluntarily made; or (3) for other lawful reasons, unless prohibited by law.

⚠️ What We Cannot Notarize

Vital Records

Birth, death, and marriage certificates must be obtained from the Bureau of Vital Statistics.

Public Records

Notaries cannot certify copies of recorded deeds, court documents, or government-filed records.

Self-Notarization

A notary cannot notarize their own signature or any document where they have a financial interest.

Legal Advice

Notaries are prohibited from explaining legal effects of documents or assisting in drafting them.
